Pomodoro

A timer that guards the break

Focus like any pomodoro app — then Sharingan does the part the others skip: it makes the rest actually happen.

Sharingan
25:0016:2407:1505:00 FocusBreak
  • Focus, your length. 25/5/15 defaults, long break every N rounds, countdown or count-up.
  • Three pomodoro sizes. Small, Normal, Big — every task (even a subtask) can pick its own, each with its own long break.
  • Speaks your language. “5 min”, “2h 30m”, “5pm”, “+15m” — type it, the clock obeys.
  • Global hotkeys. ⌃⌥Space start/pause · ⌃⌥F skip · ⌃⌥R reset · ⌃⌥L Floating widget.
  • Unskippable breaks. Full-screen glass overlay on every monitor; ⌘Q, ⌘W and ⌘Tab stay blocked.
  • Focus enforcement. Hide or force-quit distracting apps the moment a break starts — pick any installed app from the built-in catalog.

Tasks

A todo list that feeds the timer

Tasks aren't a separate app — they're what your pomodoros burn through. Queue them up and every finished session advances to the next.

Today
  • Ship the landing page#work
  • Reply to design review15:00
  • Call the banktomorrow
  • Stretch, water, look far#health
+ tomorrow p1 call the bank
  • Everything a task needs. Priorities you can extend with your own levels, tags, projects, due dates, notes — and subtasks with their own flags, estimates and sizes.
  • Natural-language quick add. “tomorrow 15:00 p1 #work report” — or “ertaga 15:00 p1 #ish hisobot”, or “明天 15:00 p1 报告”. The world's 25 most-spoken languages, all live at once, parsed as you type.
  • Paste a whole plan. Markdown or JSON — even straight out of an AI chat — becomes tasks with subtasks. Duplicates get caught, not doubled.
  • Focus queue. Each finished pomodoro advances to the next task; the break screen shows what's coming.
  • Sort, filter, snooze, repeat. Order by priority, due date or A–Z; narrow to one tag, project or level; daily, weekdays or every-N-days recurrence.
  • Know where the time went. Every session credits its task — and subtask — into a day-paged Report, in the app and the menu-bar popover.

    Eye health

    Exercises your eyes actually do

    Every break is a guided drill led by the animated eyes — and the camera (on-device, optional) checks you really did it.

    20-20-20Time left · 4:32

    Look at something far awayNow slowly to the leftAnd across to the rightBlink softly — done

    • Guided drills. 20-20-20, 8-direction gaze and blink bursts — the eyes on screen lead every step.
    • Pick your drill. Exercise chips at the top of every break — tap one and the eyes run it on demand.
    • Camera-verified. Vision face landmarks confirm your gaze and blinks happened; a retry signal if they didn't.
    • Voice guidance. Per-step TTS instructions with adjustable rate and pitch.
    • Private by design. Frames never leave your Mac; a pulsing badge whenever the camera is on.
    • Comfort extras. Screen dim, Night Shift warmth, rain/forest/lo-fi ambience, posture and water reminders.

    FAQ
    
      
    Questions, answered
    
      
    
        
    
          Is Sharingan free?
          
    Yes. Sharingan is free and open source. There is no account, no subscription and no tracking.
    
        
    
        
    
          Does camera data leave my Mac?
          
    Never. Blink and gaze detection run entirely on-device with Apple's Vision framework. The camera is optional, and a pulsing badge is always shown while it is on.
    
        
    
        
    
          Which Macs does it support?
          
    macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later, on Apple Silicon.
    
        
    
        
    
          Can I skip a break?
          
    Yes — every break screen has a skip button. Want stricter rest? Enable app blocking so distracting apps hide or quit when a break starts.
    
        
    
        
    
          How do I install it?
          
    Download Sharingan.dmg, open it and drag Sharingan to Applications. On first launch, right-click the app and choose Open — this build is not notarized.
    
        
    
        
    
          Does it sync between Macs?
          
    Not yet — iCloud sync is planned. Today everything lives in a local on-device database, and nothing is ever stored on a server.
